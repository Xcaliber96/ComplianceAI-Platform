HIPAA Security Rule - General Requirements (164.306(a))

Standard: Ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of all electronic protected health information the covered entity creates, receives, maintains, or transmits.

General Requirements:
1. Confidentiality: Protect ePHI from unauthorized access or disclosure
2. Integrity: Ensure ePHI is not improperly altered or destroyed
3. Availability: Ensure ePHI is accessible and usable when needed

Implementation Requirements:
- Implement technical safeguards to protect ePHI
- Establish security measures to prevent unauthorized access
- Protect against reasonably anticipated threats
- Protect against reasonably anticipated impermissible uses or disclosures
- Ensure workforce compliance with security requirements

Security Measures Required:
- Access controls and authentication
- Encryption where appropriate
- Audit controls and monitoring
- Integrity controls
- Transmission security
- Physical safeguards
